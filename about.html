About me

I am a Development Economist and data scientist-in-training, currently completing my MSc in Development Economics at the University of Göttingen, Germany. My work sits at the intersection of applied econometrics, development policy, agriculture, and environmental sustainability.

I enjoy working end-to-end with data: from designing empirical strategies and cleaning complex datasets, to implementing models in R, Python and Stata, and communicating results to academic, policy, and practitioner audiences.

In addition to my studies, I have experience as a student research assistant on digital health and rural development projects, including work on the digital divide, health worker adoption of TB screening tools, and geospatial analyses for development.

Research and professional interests

  • Development economics, poverty, and rural livelihoods
  • Agricultural and environmental economics, including carbon leakage and sustainable food systems
  • Data-driven policy evaluation and quasi-experimental methods
  • Digital technologies, the digital divide, and health systems in low- and middle-income countries
